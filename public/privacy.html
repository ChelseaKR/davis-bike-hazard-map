Privacy

The Davis Bike Hazard Map collects as little as possible. There are no user accounts for reporters and no third-party analytics or advertising trackers.

What a report contains

  • A hazard category, severity, and optional description you write.
  • A location. Your precise coordinate is used only on the server; the public map shows a point fuzzed to roughly a 70-metre grid so it can't identify a home or person.
  • An optional photo. Photos are EXIF-stripped and re-encoded on your device and again on the server (location/camera metadata removed), and you can blur faces and licence plates before uploading.
  • A random report id generated on your device.

Moderation & retention

Nothing is shown publicly until a moderator approves it. Your precise coordinate is kept only while a hazard is active; once it is rejected, resolved, or expires, the precise point is coarsened to the public grid. We don't sell or share personal data.

Saved-route & area alerts (optional)

If you opt in to alerts for a saved route or area, the server stores your watch geometry together with a push endpoint for your browser. The route you save is stored simplified (to roughly the same ~35‑metre precision the corridor matching needs — never your exact GPS trace), the subscription expires automatically after 180 days (using the feature again renews it), and unsubscribing deletes it immediately. Push endpoints and keys are treated as secrets and never written to logs.

Your choices

  • Delete your report. Your device holds your report id; deleting a report from “My reports” removes it locally, and the app can ask the server to delete it (record and photos) via DELETE /api/reports/<your-report-id>.
  • Open data. Approved, fuzzed hazards are published as open data (GeoJSON export, ODbL).

Questions or a deletion request you can't complete in the app? Contact the operator listed in the project README.

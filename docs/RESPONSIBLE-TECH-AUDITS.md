# Responsible-Tech Audits — Davis Bike Hazard Map

Instantiates `/STANDARDS/RESPONSIBLE-TECH-FRAMEWORK.md`. A public map of user-submitted photos and locations has real privacy and equity edges; this is where they're handled. Checklists run in CI from M0; artifacts are committed and regenerated by `make verify`.

---

## A. Ethics & responsibility
- **Worst misuse:** the map becomes a way to photograph and track identifiable people, or to over-police certain neighborhoods. → EXIF strip + blurring + moderation + a "no identifiable people" norm; coverage gaps surfaced rather than read as danger signals.
- **"Works as intended" harm:** uneven reporting makes well-covered streets look uniquely dangerous and ignored streets look safe. → Present data as reports-received, not ground truth; show coverage.
- **Non-goals:** not surveillance; not enforcement. **Auto-gated:** EXIF-strip test; export-schema test. **Review-gated:** ethics + moderation-policy review.

## B. Bias & fairness
- **Segments:** neighborhoods/areas, and report density over time.
- **Risks:** allocational bias (attention/fixes flow to loud areas), representational bias (some areas erased by under-reporting).
- **Tests:** coverage-by-area reporting; UI that distinguishes "no reports" from "safe."
- **Commitment:** never equate absence of reports with safety; show coverage. **Auto-gated:** coverage-view presence test. **Review-gated:** equity review.

## C. Privacy & data-protection
- **Data inventory:** hazard reports (type, severity, location, photo, timestamps); optional contact only if a user opts into 311 hand-off; **no accounts required to view**.
- **Photo privacy:** client-side EXIF strip + face/plate blur option, server-side EXIF strip as backstop; precise capture location may be slightly fuzzed in public display.
- **Commitment:** EXIF-clean photos, PII-free open-data export, minimal retention of any contact info. **Auto-gated:** EXIF-strip + export-schema + no-PII-in-logs tests. **Review-gated:** privacy review (location-fuzzing policy).

## D. Transparency & explainability
- **Data provenance:** each hazard shows when reported, confirmations, and status; routing explains that it avoids *reported* hazards (not all hazards).
- **Commitment:** clear lifecycle + "reported, not verified" framing; open methodology + open data. **Auto-gated:** lifecycle/labeling tests. **Review-gated:** framing review.

## E. Accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA)
- **The hard part is the map.** Every map capability has an accessible **list/table** equivalent; markers/clusters are SR-labeled; severity uses shape+color; report forms are keyboard-complete with programmatic errors.
- **Commitment:** zero `axe` violations and a committed manual walkthrough covering the list view. **Auto-gated:** axe + keyboard + list-parity tests. **Review-gated:** SR walkthrough sign-off.

## F. Security
- **Threat model:** spam/abuse submissions, malicious image uploads, location/PII leakage, 311-adapter injection.
- **Controls:** upload validation + image sanitization, rate-limiting, moderation, SAST + deps + secret scans merge-blocking, least-privilege on the 311 adapter.
- **Residual-risk register** committed in `docs/audits/`. **Auto-gated:** scanners + upload-validation tests. **Review-gated:** threat-model sign-off.
